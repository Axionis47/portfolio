Who Owns the Explanation

The model predicts. But who generates the reason — the model or the system?

There is a difference between a model making a decision and a model explaining a decision. These should almost never be the same system. The moment you let a model generate its own justification, you get plausible-sounding rationales that may have nothing to do with what actually drove the prediction.

The credit risk engine made this unavoidable. When a loan application is declined, federal law under ECOA requires you to provide specific reason codes — not "the model thinks you are risky" but "insufficient credit history" or "high debt-to-income ratio." I generate those 20 adverse action codes through SHAP feature attributions, not by asking the model to explain itself. The model predicts default probability. SHAP decomposes which features pushed that probability. The explanation is mechanistic, not generative.

The OSCE grader enforces this from the opposite direction. The model is not allowed to generate assessments from its own medical knowledge. Every score must cite the exact transcript line that justifies it. The model is the language parser, not the medical authority. The explanation is the citation, and without it, the score does not exist.

In the enterprise RAG system, the model synthesizes an answer from retrieved documents. But the attribution — which chunk said what — is tracked by the retrieval pipeline, not generated by the model. If the model claims something, the system can verify whether any retrieved chunk actually contains that claim. The model writes the paragraph; the system owns the evidence trail.

The causal inference agent has a subtler version. The model selects an estimation method and produces a notebook. But the critique loop does not ask the model "why did you pick this method?" It re-examines the dataset independently and checks whether the method's assumptions hold against the data. The justification comes from the verification, not from the model's post-hoc reasoning.

If you let the model explain itself, you get a story. If you force the system to explain the model, you get evidence.

Deterministic Boundaries Don't Negotiate

Some decisions should never touch a model. Period.

There is a category of decisions where the correct answer is not probabilistic, not context-dependent, and not open to interpretation. Access control. Regulatory compliance. Structural constraints. These are deterministic. They do not belong anywhere near a model's judgment.

The enterprise RAG system has three layers of access control: index-time boolean flags, retrieval-time ABAC policy evaluation, and post-retrieval PDP filtering. None of these layers involve a model. The model synthesizes text from whatever chunks survive the filter. But the decision of which chunks it sees is made by YAML policy rules evaluated in 0.004ms. I tested 36 files across 12 ABAC rules and got zero cross-role leakage. If I had let the model decide who sees what — even with a system prompt saying "do not show HR data to engineers" — I would be one creative prompt away from a data breach.

The credit risk engine is the same pattern applied to regulation. ECOA requires specific adverse action codes when you decline a loan. The 4/5 rule defines what constitutes disparate impact. These are not judgment calls. The model predicts probability of default. But the fairness monitoring — demographic parity, equalized odds, disparate impact ratios — is computed deterministically per batch. A model that "thinks" it is being fair is not compliant. A system that measures fairness mathematically is.

The OSCE grader applies this to structure. The 12-section rubric, the scoring criteria, the requirement that every assessment must have a citation — these are hard constraints enforced in code, not suggestions in a prompt. The model works within those boundaries. It cannot decide to skip a section, invent a criterion, or score without evidence. The architecture makes non-compliance structurally impossible.

Even in the BMS firmware at Sirius, the same principle holds at the hardware level. The 21.0V undervoltage cutoff, the 6A overcurrent limit, the 55C thermal threshold — these are deterministic boundaries in the state machine. The system does not "estimate" whether the battery is safe. It checks a number against a threshold. Some decisions should be boring.

The most important engineering decisions are the ones you refuse to let the model make.

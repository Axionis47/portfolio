A few things I got wrong first, then wrote down.

Short notes on where to draw the line between what a model decides and what it doesn’t — mostly written after something went wrong once.

01
Parametric vs. Grounded Knowledge

The OSCE grader kept hallucinating clinical assessments until I locked out the model’s own knowledge.

02
Confidence is Not Calibration

03
Who Owns the Explanation

›
04
Compression Preserves What You Measure

I removed entire layers from the GNN and accuracy went up, not down.

›
05
Deterministic Boundaries

One prompt injection against the RAG system, and I knew access control could never be a model decision.

›
06
The Verification Surface

Every system I built had a different way to catch itself being wrong — none of them was “hope.”

›
07
Autonomy is a Spectrum

The GNN has zero autonomy, the causal agent has a lot — I had to learn where to draw that line each time.

08
Eval Defines the Trust Contract

I shipped things I thought worked until I built the right eval and found out they did not.
